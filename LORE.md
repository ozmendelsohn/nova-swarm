# NOVA SWARM — Lore

*(Companion to ART_STYLE.md — the lore is the "why" behind every visual rule.)*

## The Loomworld
Before anything, there was the **Weaver**, who strung the void with warp-threads
of light and wove the world as a single endless tapestry: the **Loomworld**.
Every field, sea and mountain is cloth; every living thing is embroidery that
learned to move. The Weaver dyed the land in seven great vats — the same seven
dyes that power all magic: **Ember, Frost, Volt, Void, Verdant, Adamant, and
Arcane**.

Then the Weaver finished the final knot, set the Loom spinning on its own,
and left.

## The Unraveling
Ages later, something began to chew the far edge of the tapestry. Threads came
loose. And loose thread, soaked in old dye, does not lie still — it knots
itself into hungry shapes. This is the **Swarm**: slimes of pooled dye, moths
that eat cloth, bone-things knotted from pale warp, wraiths of unthreaded
weft. They do not hate the world; they *are* the world, coming undone. Every
minute the Unraveling spreads, the knots grow larger and stranger, until the
great **Knot-Tyrants** rise — bosses woven from whole ruined provinces. The
last of them, **NOVA PRIME**, is a knot tied around a dying star: if it
tightens, the Loom stops.

## The Threadbound
The Loom defends itself. When the fray reaches a province, the tapestry
spends its oldest magic to stitch a champion out of its own fabric — a
**Threadbound** — and arms them from the **Charm Loom**: weapons embroidered
as talismans. Four Threadbound answer in this province:

- **Nova Vanguard** — woven from sky-silk and comet wake; the dash is a
  needle-stroke through the cloth itself.
- **Hexa the Witch** — embroidered backwards, knot-side out; her magic frays
  whatever it touches.
- **Bramblefoot** — a patch of living meadow that got up; the ground heals
  beneath him.
- **Gigavolt Gunner** — stitched with copper thread during a storm; never
  stopped buzzing.

A Threadbound grows by drinking **dye-gems** — droplets of world-color shed
by unmade knots (XP). With each level the Loom trusts them with more charms
(weapon slots), and at great depth of trust, with everything it has.

## Charms, Evolutions, Fusions
- A **charm** (weapon) is a talisman: archetype shape × dye color.
- Mastering a charm forces a choice of **stitch** — two evolution paths, two
  different embroideries of the same talisman.
- **Joint Evolution**: two mastered charms can be tied into a single
  **knot-charm** — the one act of weaving the Swarm cannot copy. This is why
  fusion is the loudest, brightest moment in the game.

## The land you fight on (map rules)
The battlefield is the tapestry seen up close:
- The world is sewn from **dye-fields** — provinces of cloth, each steeped in
  one of the seven dyes, patch-worked together like a quilt.
- Fields meet at **stitched seams** — visible dashed thread-lines.
- Golden **warp-threads** run through everything at long intervals: the
  Weaver's original strings, still glowing faintly. Gold = sacred (reward
  language, per ART_STYLE.md).
- Each field bears **embroidered sigils** of its dye: ember knots, frost
  stars, leaf-work, rune cross-stitch. Decorations are embroidery, never
  objects — nothing on the ground casts a shadow; only *actors* (things that
  move) float above the cloth and cast shadows.
- The tapestry is alive: hues breathe slowly, sigils glint. But the cloth is
  always dimmer than the things standing on it (layer contract).

## Tone
Folk-tale told in an arcade cabinet: warm, a little strange, never grimdark.
The Swarm is tragic, not evil. The palette grieves in color, never in grey.
